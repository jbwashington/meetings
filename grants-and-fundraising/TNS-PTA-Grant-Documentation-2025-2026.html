üìù Lessons Learned & Best Practices

Critical Lessons from August 2025 Meeting

Insurance Paperwork Failure - Lost $2,000

What Happened: Ashokan grant funding reduced from $10,000 to $8,000 due to missing bus insurance paperwork.

Lesson Learned: Document preparation is the biggest bottleneck. Insurance certificates, especially bus/transportation coverage, must be secured and maintained at all times.

Prevention: Maintain "Grant Ready" folder with all insurance certificates updated annually.

Key Insights

  • Start with "lower hanging fruit" - Focus on difficulty ratings 1-5 first
  • Principal buy-in is essential for school-based grants
  • Earlier budget meetings help with planning - October and April general meetings recommended
  • Apply to multiple grants simultaneously when possible
  • Build relationships with funders - don't just submit and disappear

Best Practices

Documentation Management

  • Create "Grant Ready" folder with all standard docs
  • Update monthly - don't wait for deadline
  • Keep original + copies - some grants want originals
  • Version control - date all documents
  • Set calendar reminders for insurance renewals

Budget Best Practices

  • Round to nearest $25 or $50 - looks professional
  • Include 10% contingency in project budgets
  • Match narratives to numbers - they must align
  • Show other funding sources - demonstrates sustainability

Application Strategy

  • Track everything meticulously
  • Keep standard documents ready year-round
  • Read guidelines twice before starting
  • Have someone else review before submission
  • Submit at least 24 hours before deadline (technical issues happen!)

Red Flags to Avoid

Red Flag Why It Matters Solution
Missing hard deadlines No extensions granted - instant disqualification Set multiple calendar reminders, submit early
Incomplete insurance docs Cost us $2,000 on Ashokan grant Maintain current certificates at all times
Lack of principal support School-based grants require administrator buy-in Meet with principal early in process
Budget deficits Shows poor financial planning Always show balanced budget
No financial reserves Raises sustainability concerns Show 3-month operating reserve
Single funding source Risk if that source disappears Demonstrate revenue diversity
Excessive admin costs Funders want money going to programs Keep administrative costs under 15%
Unrealistic timelines Suggests poor planning Build in buffer time for all activities

Progressive Education Alignment

All grant applications should emphasize TNS's progressive education values:

  • Student-centered learning approaches - Focus on individual needs and interests
  • Equity and inclusion priorities - Serving diverse learners
  • Environmental sustainability - Garden programs, outdoor learning
  • Social justice integration - Critical thinking about community issues
  • Community-based learning - Connections beyond classroom
  • Arts and creativity emphasis - Integration across curriculum
